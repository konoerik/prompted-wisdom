Suffering is not a problem to be solved. It is a fact to be met. The mistake is to treat it as an interruption, something that has gone wrong, when in truth it is the ground on which everything else stands. The Stoics knew this: Epictetus, a slave who walked with a limp, said plainly that it is not what happens to you but how you meet it that matters. The Buddhists knew it too, though they put it differently—suffering is the first noble truth, the one that begins all wisdom. Both are saying the same thing: pain is inevitable, but misery is optional.

You will suffer. That is certain. What is not certain is whether you will let suffering break you or whether you will let it teach you. The difference is not in the weight of the blow but in the strength of your response. Marcus Aurelius, writing in the middle of war and plague, reminded himself that the obstacle is the way. What stands in your path becomes the path itself. This is not a trick of language. It is a shift in how you see. When you stop fighting what is, you stop adding to the pain.

Resilience is not the absence of suffering. It is the refusal to be defined by it. The Confucians spoke of *ren*—humaneness, the capacity to endure hardship without losing your humanity. The Greeks called it *andreia*, courage, but not the kind that charges into battle. It is the quiet courage of holding your ground when the world shakes. You do not have to be fearless. You only have to be willing to feel the fear and act anyway.

There is a kind of suffering that comes from clinging—to people, to outcomes, to the way things were supposed to be. The Buddha’s answer was to let go. The Stoics’ answer was to want what you already have. Both are ways of saying: stop resisting what you cannot change. But resilience is not passivity. It is not resignation. It is the opposite. It is the decision to keep going, not because you are certain of the outcome, but because the act of going is itself the victory.

You will not avoid suffering. But you can choose how to carry it. And in that choice, you will find that you are stronger than you thought.
